    THE NEIGHBOURHOOD




    Beasley Park is a park in the Lower City of Hamilton and named after Richard Beasley, a soldier, political figure, farmer, and businessman in Upper Canada. Richard Beasley was one of Hamilton’s first settlers who came to Canada from New York in 1777. Beasley Park is bounded by Cannon Street to the north, Mary Street to the west and and Wilson Street to the south and Ferguson Avenue to the east.


    Beasley Park was originally developed in 1976. Prior to this, Beasley Park was the site of some of Hamilton’s early industries, including the Cannon Knitting Mill. In 1991, the re-development of the park was deemed a priority for revitalization of the Beasley neighbourhood. Improvements included the installation of an accessible creative play structure, a decorative fountain, concrete walkway, a community centre and a skateboarding area.
